About Me

I grew up in Nashik, looking up at dark skies that quietly nudged me towards the universe. That curiosity eventually took me to the Indian Institute of Technology Madras (IITM), and later to the National Centre for Radio Astrophysics‚ÄìTIFR (NCRA-TIFR), where I am now a PhD researcher in radio astronomy.

I am a radio astronomer working on fast radio bursts (FRBs) ‚Äì millisecond flashes of radio light from distant galaxies. My research focuses on understanding how these bursts are produced and what their local environments can tell us about the galaxies they live in.

I lead prompt follow-up observations of FRBs to study their properties across radio frequencies and carry out low-frequency interferometric studies of persistent radio sources (PRS) associated with FRBs using uGMRT and the VLA. I have also worked on constraining faint afterglow emission by stacking observations of CHIME catalogue-I FRBs.

Beyond science questions, I enjoy building the tools that make this work possible. I have been involved in the development and commissioning of a 15-m telescope at NCRA and the SWAN telescope at RRI, and I develop an automated burst-fitting pipeline while contributing to search-pipeline development within the CHIME/Slow collaboration.
